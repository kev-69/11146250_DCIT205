Level 100 Courses Structure

Upon gaining admission into the institution, Bachelor of Science in Information Technology are automatically admitted into the department. However, student who want to puruse BSc in Computer Science are tested to know whether they are deem fit for the program.

Bachelor Of Science in Information Technology

Students under this discipline take 18-21 credit hours each semester. In Level 100, the department requires students to take only 15CH and additional 3CH from the UGRCs making a total of 18. If in any case the student feels he has the capacity to take more, he is allowed to take only 3 additional credits

The course structure for BSc in IT students are fixed for both semesters. For L100 semester 1, they include;

  • DCIT101 (Introduction to Computer Science)
  • DCIT103 (Office Productivity Tools)
  • DCIT105 (Math for IT Professionals)
  • STAT111 (Introduction to Statistics 1)
  • ECON101 (Introduction to Economics 1)
  • and a UGRC(either UGRC110(Academic Writing 1) or UGRC150(Critical Thinking and Pratical Reasoning))

Bachelor Of Science in Mathematical Sciences

Unlike BSc in IT, you'll not be considered a computer science student automatically. Your results in both the 1st and 2nd semester in L100 will tell whether you fit or not. There are various other programs under this main program including BSc in Statistics, BSc in Mathematics, BSc in Acturial Science and BSc in Computer Science.

Students who want to pursue BSc in Computer Science, minor-major, major-minor or single major will take the following courses in the first semester;

  • DCIT101 (Introduction to Computer Science)
  • DCIT103 (Office Productivity Tools)
  • MATH121 (Algebra and Trigonometry)
  • MATH123 (Vectors and Geometry)
  • STAT111 (Introduction to Statistics 1)
  • and a UGRC(either UGRC110(Academic Writing 1) or UGRC150(Critical Thinking and Pratical Reasoning))
